Basboosa

Middle Eastern Semolina Coconut Cake

Four slices of golden-brown basboosa, each with an almond on top, on a plate.

Basboosa or Harrissa is a Middle Eastern dessert made from semolina and coconut, drenched in a sweet rose water syrup. Some add tahini to give it a tangy tase, or honey to add some earthy sweetness, but I prefer the simpler method of just regular sugar and no tahini.

This dessert is both easy and a crowd-pleaser. No eggs and simple ingredients that you can just throw together? Yes please! The hardest part of this recipe is not eating all of the blanched almonds (which if placed evenly, will give the appearence of hard work).

Ingredients

For the Cake:

  • 1 cup of course Semolina
  • 1/2 cup of shreded coconut
  • 1 tsp of baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp of salt
  • 1/2 cup of plain yogurt
  • 2 tbs of sugar (or honey)
  • 16 to 24 pieces of blanched almond halves
  • Unsalted butter

For the Syrup:

  • 1 1/2 cup of sugar
  • 1 cup water
  • 1 to 2 tsp of rose water (to taste)
  • 1 tsp of lemon juice

Steps

  1. Preheat Oven to 375 F. Butter a 9 inch pan.
  2. Mix all of the ingredients of the cake together, until all ingredients are combined, it wil resemble a moldable paste. Add more yogurt if it is too dry.
  3. Put the cake in the pan and superficially cut diamond or square shapes and butter the top. Press an almond into each square. Let it bake for about 20 minutes or until it is golden brown
  4. While baking, put all of the ingredients for the syrup in a saucepan on the stove. Then bring onto high heat until it boils. Then simmer on low heat until the syrup coats the back of a spoon. Turn off heat.
  5. Take out cake and pour syrup evenly over the cake while its still hot. Allow cake to cool for at least 30 mintues before serving.
  6. Enjoy!
